Interior Design Concept for Modern New Home

A young family bought the newly built house from a property builder. This house is minimal and modern, but its decor was more suitable for periodic houses with high ceilings. The new owners wanted a modern interior design to complement the property's exciting architecture. We replaced the predictable and unpredictable look used by the developers with a design that looks contemporary and personal at the same time, tailored to the owners' social lifestyle. Large picture windows in each room overlook the ancient forests, so inviting nature was also important. This house should be a house with a conscious but calm design, which wears its luxury items and finishes slowly.

About the Modern New Home

It has a generous open-plan living area, three bedrooms all with en suite bathrooms, an extra guest bathroom with a hidden shower, and a TV room. It is located in a generous garden on a private development in Golsar, Rasht. It covers an area of 200 square meters with ceilings of 2.7 meters and each room has floor-to-ceiling windows. Kebony, a viable alternative to tropical hardwood, has been widely used in construction.

Space Layout in Modern New Home

The original layout was correct, but we carefully adjusted some of the details to make the most of the space and make sure the house really suits its owners and their lifestyle. The living room was optimized to create a large living area with a corner sofa and a separate study area. We also designed a modern fireplace with easy-to-use bioethanol fire. The flexible dining area has a table that is suitable for family meals, but when friends and relatives visit it, it can be a party for 10 people. We added a sofa bed to the TV room and turned the dressing room into a guest bathroom. Shower hidden behind a custom glass and iron partition made in Italy. We also redesigned the shower room to make better use of its space and installed a bathtub suitable for children.

The flooring

Less luxury, with plenty of heat, texture and natural materials, is at the heart of this home's appearance. That's why we replaced the original gray floor tiles - so cold! - With wide engineered oak boards, on underfloor heating.

The bathrooms

In the guest shower room, black irons point to the window frame of the house. We combined it with Beton Cire, a good type of plaster made in France, on the walls. The other three private bathrooms feature natural wood flooring, toilet units and decorative panels for warmth. In the baby bath, we used Domus handmade red tile tiles for fun. The combination of materials in all bathrooms - concrete, iron, wood and glass - keeps them modern, but also cozy.

The walls

We continued with a neutral color scheme, but added walls of Philip Jefferson natural fiber wallpaper to create a cozy atmosphere, introduce texture, and connect the outside with the inside.

The furniture

We have thousands of contacts in the world of design and we have searched for them to find the right furniture for this house. We sourced parts and fittings from more than 30 different suppliers in 15 countries. The walnut coffee table is made by a Canadian craftsman, while the armchair is made by Italian furniture maker Moroso. For a custom edge, we also covered some of the pieces in a fabric that was specifically chosen to complete the design. For example, a corner sofa from the Scandinavian design team Norr11 is covered with quadrat fabric. In the master bedroom, we changed the clean and modern look by adding side tables from the 1950s by Osvaldo Borsani, found in an old London exhibition. In the study area, we used De La Espada furniture - its exceptional design and quality match the beauty of this house.

The colour scheme

There are natural colors throughout and each room has its own palette. The living room looks to be inspired by autumn, with khaki colors, wine accents and black colors tied to the window frames. In the guest bedroom, light blue, salmon and green shades are combined. The baby's bedroom is mostly neutral, but a wall is covered with a large floral pattern to introduce the "wow" factor. Complementing dark rose curtains, to create an unexpected and not childish design. The master bedroom is the most colorful bedroom, with natural brown wood fiber wallpaper along with a bed covered with dark gray fabric. The light mustard color of its tubes comes on the velvet sofa and all the colors are balanced with the pale walls and floors to create an original and at the same time soothing design.

The lighting

The house receives a lot of light through large windows and floodlights installed throughout, but we have complemented this light with a combination of ceiling, wall and floor lamps that zoning the space and creating a more intimate atmosphere. Beautiful round pendants by Kenneth Cobonpue, handmade in the Philippines, give a dramatic effect to the dining area, while floor lamps by Matthew Hilton are the best color for the study area. Its sleek ghost does not detract from the outstanding artwork of Canadian artist Sandra Lamb, and its slim, pleated shade appears to mirror washed linen curtains in the background.
